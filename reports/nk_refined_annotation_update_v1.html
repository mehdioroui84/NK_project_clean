Batch strategy re-evaluation

Batch correction depends on whether we ask a global or local question

The clean comparison no longer uses an arbitrary overall score. We show direct metrics for dataset mixing, assay mixing, and NK-state preservation.
  • ASW mixing is the global view: do batches separate across the latent space?
  • kNN mixing is the local view: can nearest neighbors still recover dataset or assay?
  • NK_State ASW/kNN are biology-preservation guardrails, shown separately.
  • For clustering and discovery, local kNN mixing is the stricter test.

Absolute success scores, not relative min-max ranks. Assay-only has strongest global ASW mixing; composite improves local kNN mixing. Biology is reported as separate NK_State ASW and kNN metrics.

Metric definitions

How the batch and biology scores were defined

Silhouette for a label set

si = bi-ai max(ai,bi)

Here ai is the mean distance to cells with the same label and bi is the nearest mean distance to another label.

NK_State ASW preservation

SNK = 1+ 1 n ∑i=1n si(yi) 2

Higher means known NK states remain separated in the latent space.

Batch ASW mixing

MASW(B) = 1- | 1 n ∑i=1n si(Bi) |

Computed within NK_State strata for either B = dataset_id or B = assay_clean. Higher means less global batch separation.

NK_State kNN preservation

ANK-kNN = 1n ∑i=1n I (y^i=yi)

ŷi is the majority NK_State among the k nearest neighbors of cell i.

Batch kNN mixing

AB-kNN = 1n ∑i=1n I (B^i=Bi)

B̂i is the majority dataset or assay among nearest neighbors, evaluated within NK_State strata.

Baseline-adjusted batch kNN score

MkNN = 1- max(0,AB-kNN-Abase) 1-Abase

Higher means batch is no more locally predictable than the within-NK_State majority-batch baseline.

Reference model behavior

SCANVI performs well overall, but its errors highlight where biology may be unresolved

What this told us

  • Major states are learned with high validation performance.
  • Some errors concentrate in specific regions rather than being random.
  • Held-out/zero-shot behavior flagged classes like `Unknown_Lung_3` as poorly learned because almost all examples were in held-out data.
Decision: do not force extra batch correction. Use the assay-only latent space as the production baseline and use SCVI for substructure discovery.
Discovery setup

SCVI Leiden clustering gave us a controlled way to ask: how much structure is hidden inside the labels?

Resolution tradeoff: ARI/NMI compare Leiden clusters with manual `NK_State`. Silhouette is computed on SCVI latent coordinates using Leiden cluster IDs, not dataset or assay batch labels.

Resolution 0.4 was selected as the working balance: not too coarse, not too fragmented.

We selected `leiden_0_4`: 21 clusters, interpretable structure, and enough resolution to split broad or unknown states.

Interpretation and caveats

What this v1 annotation means, and what it does not mean yet

What we can say now

  • The existing annotation captures major NK biology.
  • SCVI latent structure exposes meaningful substructure inside broad states.
  • Pairwise DE supports new labels in cytokine-stimulated, lung, and tissue-resident transitional compartments.
  • The mapping is clear enough to use as the next working annotation.

What remains cautious

  • Some labels are tissue or dataset enriched; that can be real biology, but should be tracked.
  • Validation-set labels are not enough to retrain SCANVI yet.
  • The mature-cytotoxic branch still deserves a later dedicated review.
  • Full-dataset annotation must come before the final retraining pass.
We should treat this as `NK_State_refined_validation_v1`: locked enough to proceed, not final enough to stop validating.
